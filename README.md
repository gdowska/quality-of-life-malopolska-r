# Quality of Life Analysis in Małopolskie Voivodeship

Academic project focused on analyzing spatial differences in quality of life across districts in the Małopolskie Voivodeship using statistical methods in R.

## Project goal

The goal of the project was to compare districts in Małopolskie Voivodeship in terms of quality of life and to identify groups of districts with similar socio-economic characteristics.

The analysis was based on district-level indicators describing infrastructure, access to public services, labour market conditions, culture, health and economic activity.

## Scope of the analysis

- data preparation and exploratory data analysis,
- analysis of missing values and outliers,
- descriptive statistics and visualization of variables,
- correlation analysis using Spearman correlation,
- construction of synthetic quality-of-life rankings,
- comparison of districts using Hellwig’s method,
- comparison of districts using the standardized sums method,
- comparison of ranking results,
- clustering of districts using k-medoids,
- hierarchical clustering using Ward’s method,
- interpretation of spatial differences between districts.

## Methods

- exploratory data analysis,
- variable standardization,
- Hellwig’s method,
- standardized sums method,
- synthetic development measure,
- Spearman correlation,
- k-medoids clustering,
- Manhattan distance,
- hierarchical clustering,
- Ward’s method,
- Calinski-Harabasz index.
